Overall Company Policy (Sec. 2.1)

Reefership's international team of seafarers and shore staff is fully committed to the consistent operation of a safe and efficient fleet of refrigerated ships and container units, maintaining high quality standards, and actively protecting the environment.

  • Personnel Safety: Prevent accidental loss of life or injury to seafarers or third parties onboard.
  • Vessel Safety: Prevent accidents that can cause damage to the ship or other ships.
  • Cargo Protection: Prevent all forms of cargo damage.
  • Environmental Protection: Protect the environment and prevent air and sea pollution.
  • Compliance: Ensure adherence to all International, National, Flag State, Class Society, and Company Rules and Regulations.
QSMS Objectives (Sec. 2.2)

The Quality and Safety Management System (QSMS) has five main objectives that form the basis of all activities related to safety, protection of the environment, and the quality of services provided.

  • Personnel Safety: Prevent accidental loss of life or injury to seafarers or third parties onboard.
  • Vessel Safety: Prevent accidents that can cause damage to the ship or other ships.
  • Cargo Protection: Prevent all forms of cargo damage.
  • Environmental Protection: Protect the environment and prevent air and sea pollution.
  • Compliance: Ensure adherence to all International, National, Flag State, Class Society, and Company Rules and Regulations.
Environmental Policy (Sec. 2.3)

Environmental stewardship is a core operational issue at RMS, integral to our concept of quality (DOLE label), and supported by a commitment to zero-tolerance for pollution.

  • Core Philosophy: Environmental protection is an operational issue and an integral part of Reefership's concept of quality.
  • Approach: RMS takes a preventative approach to identify and address environmental issues before they become a concern.
  • Pollution: There is a zero tolerance towards water pollution, and necessary disposal facilities will be provided.
  • Responsibility: Every employee has a basic responsibility to comply with all environmental rules and regulations.
Fatigue Management Policy (Sec. 2.4)

The Fatigue Management Policy ensures safety by strictly adhering to rest requirements, even when facing operational pressures like bad weather or loading delays.

  • Recognition: Management recognizes that circumstances like bad weather or delays may affect rest periods.
  • Overriding Rule: No commercial considerations are allowed to override the rest requirements.
  • Standard: Requirements contained in the STCW 95 amendment, section A-VIII/1 (Fitness for Duty) must be strictly followed.
  • Application: This rule applies equally to the Master and all other officers and watch keepers on duty.
Drug and Alcohol Policy (Sec. 2.5)

RMS maintains a stringent policy with zero tolerance for illegal drugs and strict limits on alcohol consumption and testing to ensure fitness for duty.

The policy outlines:

  • Illegal Drugs: Zero tolerance for use or possession of illegal drugs; infringement results in immediate termination.
  • Legal Risk: The Company will not defend any seafarer prosecuted for infringing worldwide drug trafficking/use laws.
  • Alcohol Restriction: Seafarers will not consume any alcohol within 4 hours of going on duty, whether at sea or in port.
  • Testing & Threshold: Random testing may be conducted; an alcohol concentration of () when on duty will result in termination.
Pets on Board Vessels (Sec. 2.6)

The Company maintains a clear prohibition against carrying any pets onboard vessels to uphold health, safety, and operational standards.

The policy states:

  • Prohibition: It is the strict policy of the Company that no pets (including dogs, cats, parrots, etc.) will be carried on board the vessels.
Data Protection Policy (Sec. 2.7)

RMS has a strict Data Protection Policy under which rights of crew members and obligations of the company, with respect to privacy and personal information processing and protection are clearly established.

  • Purpose: Personal information is collected only for ensuring work effectiveness and for submissions required for international travel and certification requirements.
  • Scope: The data processed has a limited risk to the privacy of the individual.

Reference: The detailed policy is available in the Main Manual Appendix 10.

Social Media Policy (Sec. 2.8)

RMS has a very strict Social Media Policy to which all individuals working at all levels on board the Company vessels must always comply in order to safeguard the Company's reputation.

  • Compliance: All individuals working at all levels must comply to safeguard the Company's reputation.
  • Restrictions: Describes restrictions on the use of Social Media during both work and non-work hours, on Company-supplied or personal devices.
  • Device Prohibition: RMS prohibits carrying and use of personal devices to the place of work while on duty.

Reference: The detailed policy is available in the Main Manual Appendix 11
