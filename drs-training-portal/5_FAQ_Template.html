FAQs

A. The Core Mission – Safety, ISM & Confidence
1. What is the purpose of this training portal?
The portal serves as an integrated learning environment designed to build confidence, streamline ISM compliance, and raise safety awareness across the fleet.
2. What’s the difference between my mandatory Certification (CoC) and the Training provided here? ?
That’s a great question. Certification (like your CoC or endorsements) proves you've met a minimum STCW (Standards of Training, Certification and Watchkeeping for Seafarers) to hold your rank. Training, on the other hand, is about building practical competence and confidence. Certification is the license to drive, but our training is the continuous practice that ensures you can handle the stickiest situations without hesitation. You’re certified, but are you trained to handle the unexpected? That's what we focus on.
3. Why is this Portal focused so intensely on Safety and reducing accidents?
The maritime industry on the whole is trying to achieve "Zero Accidents". This Portal exists because the root cause of most accidents and incidents is human error, often due to lack of practical knowledge or preparedness. By focusing on targeted, recurrent training, we aim to eliminate confusion, improve decision-making speed and build a "Safety First" culture.
4. Does this portal replace onboard training?
No. It enhances onboard learning by standardizing content and making training accessible anytime, anywhere.
5. What is the link between the ISM Code, our QSMS and this Training Portal?
The ISM Code dictates the legal WHAT (mandatory safety).
Our SMS defines the company's HOW (documented procedures for compliance).
The QSMS (Quality & Safety Management System) is our enhanced commitment, integrating both safety and continuous quality improvement.

6. How will these courses actually help me when I’m faced with a sticky situation on board?
The courses are designed around real-life scenarios, not just theory. The goal is to move knowledge from your head to your hands. When you face an emergency—a machinery breakdown, fire, or critical maneuver—you won’t have time to look up the procedure. Our training builds the automatic response, giving you the confidence and clarity to act decisively and correctly under pressure.
7. Is this training mandatory for all Officers and Ratings?
Yes. To maintain our operational standards and ensure compliance with the QSMS, all personnel are required to complete the training modules assigned to their rank and vessel type.
B. Portal Access – The 2-Step Security & Setup
8. How do I Register?
A communique will be sent by email to all vessels and personnel, with an access link. This link will take you to the landing page of the Portal. The registration process is as follows:

  1. Click on the button marked, "Go to Training".
  2. Fill up the registration form with your details (This is a single tabbed form - One tab to register and the other tab for Login).
  3. After approval from the administrator you will receive an email with a Login Password and ID.

Use the Registration/Login page to enter your password and ID to enter the main Training Portal.

9. Why is the login process so strict? Why do I have to register and then wait for an email?
We operate a tight, secure system for two critical reasons: Cyber Security and Content Integrity. We need to verify that you are an active employee before granting access to our proprietary QSMS and training materials. This two-step process ensures unauthorized access is impossible, protecting our digital assets and sensitive operational procedures.
10. I’ve forgotten my password. How do I get a new one?
Write to the Administrator at "drstrainingresponse@gmail.com" with you Full Name, Rank & email id. The Subject line must start with: "Forgotten Password" followed by your full name.
You will receive your password and login id via return mail shortly"
11. What can I do on the main Portal once I’m logged in?
The main Portal is your operational and administrative hub. Here you can:

  • Access and understand how to fill out QSMS familiarization forms (The actual forms will be filled on the ship)
  • View your Rank-Specific Job Description (JD).
  • Use the ‘Courses’ menu to view and apply for training modules.
  • Access this help/FAQ section.
    • 12. Where are the Job Descriptions (JDs) and what are the reference links I keep seeing?
    The JDs for every rank are located under the individual Department eg. The main menu of "Deck" will have JD of Master, C/Off, etc under the Rank submenu item. We have embedded direct, relevant links to reference materials within the JD page itself. This is designed so you can quickly clarify a procedure without having to leave the JD page, keeping your focus where it needs to be.
    13. How do I complete my QSMS Familiarization Forms?
    The QSMS Familiarization Forms are available under the dedicated Rank JD. These forms are mandatory steps after joining or changing vessels. The forms in the Portal are for making you familiar with them. Once you get on board you will fill them after actually going through the checklists. They cannot be submitted through the Portal.

    The main Portal contains introductory information, like Job Descriptions, for all ranks for transparency and general reference. However, the actual Training Classrooms are strictly restricted rank-wise and you will not be able to see the training content of any other rank.

    C. Training Access – Enrollment & The Classroom
    17. I see the 'Courses' menu. If I apply, does that automatically enroll me?
    No, not quite. The 'Courses' menu provides a list of available Deck, Engine, and Miscellaneous modules. Clicking "Apply" sends an enrollment request to the Training Administrator. This is the second step in our access process.
    18. What happens after I click ‘Apply’ for a course?
    The Administrator will review your application to confirm the course is appropriate for your rank, experience, and current assignment. They will then contact you to finalize the enrollment and, if approved, send you a separate, unique Class ID and password for the Google Classroom platform.
    19. Why do I need a separate Class ID and password for the training material?
    This is a core component of our tight security model. The actual, proprietary training material (videos, quizzes, assessments) resides in specialized Google Classrooms. This second layer of authentication ensures that only specific, approved individuals—for example, only Third Officers—can access that rank's specific, sensitive content.
    20. My Rank is Chief Engineer. Can I access the training material for the Second Officer?
    No, you cannot. All training content in the Google Classrooms is strictly controlled and rank-restricted. A Chief Engineer's Class ID will only grant access to Chief Engineer modules, ensuring focus and preventing accidental exposure to irrelevant or restricted material.
    21. What are the three types of training: Online, Workshop, and Hybrid?
    • Online: Fully self-paced training done through the Google Classroom, using videos, text, and online quizzes.
    • Workshop: Face-to-face, instructor-led sessions (either in-house or externally), typically for practical skill development.
    • Hybrid: A combination, where you complete the theoretical knowledge and quizzes online first, and may be followed by practical, hands-on Workshop session.
    22. How much time will the Online courses take, and can I pause them?
    The time commitment varies by module, but it will be clearly stated when you enroll. All online content is self-paced. You can log in and out as needed, and the platform will save your progress, allowing you to complete modules around your duties and watch hours.
    23. Do I need special software or equipment for the Google Classrooms?Do I need special software or equipment for the Google Classrooms?
    No. You only need a stable internet connection and a standard web browser (Chrome, Firefox, etc.) or the free Google Classroom app on your mobile device. All material, including videos and quizzes, is accessible directly through the platform.
    24. How do I know when a new course is available for my rank?
    New courses and refresher requirements will be announced via company email, and they will automatically appear on the Course List accessible through the main Portal.
    25.If I enroll in a Workshop or Hybrid course, how will I know the dates and location?
    Once the Administrator approves your application, they will contact you directly via email or phone to confirm the specific dates, times, and location details for the in-person component.
    D. Confidence, Assessment & Support
    26. How do the quizzes and final assessments work?
    The assessments are designed to test your practical understanding of the safety procedures, not just your ability to memorize facts. The quizzes are interactive and appear throughout the modules, while a final assessment ensures you've achieved the necessary standard before receiving credit for the course. Assessment results are system-generated and periodically verified by the Training Department.
    27. Do I get a Certificate when I complete a course?
    Yes. Upon successful completion of a module (including achieving the passing grade on the final assessment), you will be issued a Certificate of Training Completion, which will be logged in your official training record.
    28. What happens if I fail an assessment?
    This training is about ensuring competence, so don’t worry! If you do not pass, you will be given guidance on the specific areas you need to revisit. You will then have a set number of attempts to retake the assessment after reviewing the material.
    29. Will my completed training records follow me to my next vessel?
    Absolutely. All your training and certification records are permanently stored in the company database and are linked to your employee profile, ensuring a continuous record of your professional development throughout your career with us.

    We constantly review and update content based on your valuable input.
